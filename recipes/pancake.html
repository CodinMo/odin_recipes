French pancake

Description

French pancake or "crepes" are a versatile and delicate French dish, made from a simple batter of flour, eggs, milk, and a pinch of salt. This thin batter is poured onto a hot, lightly greased skillet and spread evenly to create a thin, lacy pancake. Once the edges begin to lift and the underside turns golden brown, the crepe is flipped to cook the other side. The result is a light, slightly crisp crepe that serves as a perfect canvas for both sweet and savory fillings.

Crepes can be enjoyed in a variety of ways, reflecting their adaptability. Sweet crepes are often filled with ingredients like fresh fruit, Nutella, whipped cream, or a sprinkle of sugar and lemon juice. Savory crepes might include fillings such as ham and cheese, sautéed vegetables, or eggs. Whether served as a breakfast treat, a dessert, or a main course, crepes are beloved for their simplicity and endless customization options, making them a staple in many culinary traditions worldwide.

Ingredients

  • Flour: These basic French crêpes start with a cup of all-purpose flour.
  • Eggs: Eggs act as a binder, which means they help hold the batter together.
  • Milk: Milk adds moisture and keeps the crêpes tender.
  • Water: Water helps thin the batter.
  • Salt: A pinch of salt enhances the overall flavor.
  • Butter: Butter lends richness and moisture to the crêpes.

Steps

  1. Whisk the milk, water, eggs, and salt.
  2. Gradually whisk in the flour and butter.
  3. Scoop the batter onto a hot griddle.
  4. Cook until lightly browned on the bottom.
  5. Flip and continue cooking until done on both sides.
